## Abstract
Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM) is associated with significant microvascular and cardiovascular complications, including diabetic kidney disease and cardiac autonomic neuropathy. Microalbuminuria is an early marker of diabetic nephropathy, while QTc interval prolongation reflects autonomic dysfunction and increased cardiovascular risk. Emerging evidence suggests a coexistence of these abnormalities, possibly due to shared mechanisms such as endothelial dysfunction and inflammation. This study aims to investigate the association between microalbuminuria and QTc interval prolongation in patients with T2DM to facilitate early risk identification, improved monitoring, and timely intervention for adverse renal and cardiovascular outcomes.
